Meet the Makers

Our Story & Dedication

Curing culinary experiences that blend traditional cheesemongering craft with modern styling.

A Decent Obsession

The Art of the Graze

L'Aura Grazing was born out of a simple love for hosting dinner parties where the central board became the fireplace around which stories were shared.

Founded in 2021 by a pairing stylist and a certified cheesemonger, we set out to change the way catering is viewed. Instead of chafing dishes and static platters, we wanted to build visual feasts that invite interaction, exploration, and sensory play.

Every element on our boards is placed with intention. The tanginess of the pickled grapes cuts through the buttery fat of the triple-cream brie. The smoky, complex saltiness of the spicy coppa pairs wonderfully with the sweet crunch of honeycomb. We balance flavors, textures, and colors to create edible masterworks.

Ethical Sourcing

Sourced from Local Dairies & Farms

We pride ourselves on direct, transparent supply lines. We know exactly where our ingredients originate. Our honey comes from a rooftop cooperative in the heart of the city; our soft goat cheeses are handmade on a farm in Wisconsin; and our prosciutto is cured by a family-owned salumeria in Italy.

By buying from small-scale farms, we ensure peak freshness, seasonal variability, and support for local sustainable agriculture.

Our Curation Standards

Every board we construct follows three absolute culinary mandates to guarantee visual elegance and exceptional flavor profile.

Small-Batch Exclusives

We only select cheese and cured meats from independent family creameries and artisan curing houses, ensuring rich flavors unavailable in mass markets.

100% Organic Produce

From fresh figs and local honeycomb to organic grapes and hand-picked flowers, every decorative element is fully edible, organic, and clean.

Cold-Chain Preserved

All ingredients are kept strictly temperature-controlled during sorting, preparation, transport, and setup to maximize taste and food safety.

Common Inquiries

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our cheese delivery, shelf life, and setup operations.

For custom boards and standard platters, we recommend ordering at least 48 hours in advance. For large event grazing tables and weddings, please book 2 to 4 weeks in advance to secure your date.

We take food allergies very seriously. We offer gluten-free cracker substitutions, nut-free preparations, and complete vegetarian or vegan/dairy-free boards. Please specify any dietary requirements during your checkout or inquiry call.

For optimal flavor and food safety, we advise that cheese and charcuterie boards sit at room temperature for no more than 2 to 3 hours. Keep them out of direct sunlight and in a cool, air-conditioned room until serving time.

We provide all the necessary food-safe display boards, risers, and decorative elements (like floral arrangements). However, the client is responsible for providing the actual physical table or surface where the grazing station will be built.

For our Grand Feast tables and large bespoke orders, our on-site setup and styling fee is included. For smaller delivery boxes and individual platters, there is a delivery fee based on mileage.
